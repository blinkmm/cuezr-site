A live link from PowerPoint to your StreamDeck.

If you already use OSC Point with Bitfocus Companion, the next version of CUEZR will drop its visualizer right into your shows — leaving your existing Companion workflow completely intact.

Origin

Actually, the Companion version came first.

CUEZR started life as a fully functional Companion-based tool — every piece of slide, build, and video data was pulled through Companion triggers and displayed in a dashboard. It worked beautifully for operators who already lived inside Companion.

But Companion itself can be daunting for folks coming in fresh. Triggers, variables, feedback loops, internal actions — it's a lot of setup for "I just want to see what slide we're on."

So the direct OSC Point version — the one you're running today — was built to cut out that middle step. Point CUEZR at your PowerPoint machines and it just works.

What's Next

Bringing it full circle.

Now that the direct version is stable, the Companion integration is coming back. A handful of triggers inside Companion will listen to the same OSC Point feed and pass that data into CUEZR — meaning you get the CUEZR visualizer and keep every existing StreamDeck button, internal action, and automation you've already built.

A downloadable .companionconfig file will ship with the next release so you can one-click import every trigger into your existing Companion instance. Five-minute setup, no manual trigger creation.
